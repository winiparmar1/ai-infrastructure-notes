The $27B Deal — What's Actually Happening
Part 1: The $12B "Dedicated Capacity" Block
The $12 billion is allocated for dedicated, high-density AI clusters across Nebius's expanding global footprint — including a new 1.2-gigawatt "AI Factory" in Missouri and several European locations. FinancialContent
What "dedicated" means in plain terms: Meta is essentially reserving entire clusters — not sharing them with anyone else. They get the raw hardware, the interconnects, the whole environment, purpose-built for their workloads. Think of it like leasing an entire data center floor vs. renting a few servers.
Nebius will begin delivering this capacity starting in early 2027 — built on one of the first large-scale deployments of the NVIDIA Vera Rubin platform. The Motley Fool

Part 2: The $15B "Backstop" or Elastic Block — This is the Smart Part
Nebius intends to sell that capacity to third-party customers first — with Meta buying whatever remains. It's a smart structure: Meta gets guaranteed access to frontier compute, while Nebius keeps the flexibility to monetize overflow capacity to startups and enterprises. FinancialContent
Why this is genius for Nebius as a sales story:

Nebius gets to sell the same capacity twice in priority order — first to its regular enterprise/startup customers at market rate, and if any is left, Meta buys it
Meta essentially functions as a financial floor — Nebius is never sitting on unsold compute
For an enterprise AE at Nebius, this also means: even the "leftover" capacity has a buyer locked in. That's an incredibly strong pitch to smaller customers — you're literally selling on the same infrastructure that Meta is training Llama on


Why Vera Rubin Specifically?
Vera Rubin is expected to deliver substantial gains in AI training and inference performance, driven by new GPU architectures, higher-bandwidth memory, and next-generation interconnects that link tens of thousands of accelerators into massive AI superclusters. Nebius
The Vera Rubin NVL144 GPUs are built on a cutting-edge 3nm process and are engineered specifically for "agentic AI" — systems capable of complex reasoning and multi-step planning. FinancialContent
The competitive moat this creates for Nebius:
Nebius's priority access to Vera Rubin is rooted partly in Yandex's historic status as one of Nvidia's earliest large-scale infrastructure partners FinancialContent — this isn't just a commercial relationship, it's a hardware pipeline advantage that competitors can't easily replicate.

The Bigger Picture — Why Meta Is Doing This
Meta wants to be training its next generation of Llama models on the most advanced chips available, not the previous generation. Given how competitive the AI race has become, a six-month hardware advantage over rivals matters. FinancialContent
Meta had been aggressively scouting for alternatives to the traditional Big Three cloud providers — Amazon, Microsoft, and Google — seeking more specialized, high-density environments for its AI models. Nebius
And the scale of Meta's AI spend makes this deal almost small in context: Meta's AI-related capital expenditure is projected to reach up to $135 billion in 2026 alone, with external cloud commitments exceeding $40 billion since October 2025. Yahoo Finance
